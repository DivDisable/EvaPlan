Weightlifting Training Program

Power Cycle Preparation

Program Overview

This program is designed to prepare an athlete for a power cycle by focusing on strength, mobility, and technique. The goal is to build a solid foundation for future lifting cycles.

The program includes the following guidelines:

  • Frequency: 3 sessions per week
  • Focus: Squats, Deadlifts, Presses, Accessory Work
  • Goal: Build strength and enhance movement patterns for power lifting

Warm-Up Phases

The warm-up should take approximately 15-20 minutes, focusing on mobility and activation. The typical warm-up will include the following phases:

  1. Activation (5-10 minutes) – Light cardio (rowing or cycling) to elevate the heart rate.
  2. Mobility (5 minutes) – Dynamic stretching for hips, shoulders, and ankles.
  3. Movement Prep (5 minutes) – Bodyweight exercises like squats and lunges.
  4. Strength-Specific Activation (5-10 minutes) – Light sets of the main lifts (e.g., squats or deadlifts).

Weekly Training Program

This program consists of 3 training sessions per week, with a mix of lower and upper body strength training.

Monday: Lower Body Strength

Warm-Up: 5-10 minutes of rowing, mobility drills, and bodyweight squats.

Main Lifts:

  • Back Squat: 5 sets of 8-10 reps @ 60-70% of 1RM (focus on form and technique)
  • Overhead squat: 4 sets of 6 reps @ 40% of 1RM
  • Deadlift: 4 sets of 6 reps @ 65% of 1RM

Accessory Work:

  • Hip Thrusts: 3 sets of 12 reps (100kg)
  • Core Work: Planks 3 sets x 30 seconds
  • Crunch: Planks 3 sets x 12 reps (10kg plate)

Wednesday: Upper Body Strength

Warm-Up: Light cycling or rowing for 5 minutes, dynamic shoulder stretches.

Main Lifts:

  • Shoulder Press: 5 sets of 8-10 reps @ 50-60% of 1RM
  • Pull-Ups: 4 sets of 6-8 reps (2kg)
  • Bench Press: 5 sets of 8-10 reps @ 50-60% of 1RM

Accessory Work:

  • Dumbbell Rows: 3 sets of 10 reps per arm (24kg)
  • Triceps Dips: 3 sets of 8 reps
  • Core Work: Russian Twists 3 sets of 20 reps

Friday: Full Body Strength & Conditioning

Warm-Up: 5-10 minutes of light cardio, dynamic stretching.

Main Lifts:

  • Front Squat: 5 sets of 8 reps @ 60-70% of 1RM
  • Snatch Deadlift: 4 sets of 8 reps @ 60% of 1RM

Accessory Work:

  • Kettlebell Swings: 3 sets of 15 reps
  • Step-Ups: 3 sets of 12 reps per leg
  • Core Work: Hanging Leg Raises 3 sets of 12 reps

Program Summary

This program provides a balanced approach to strength training, mobility, and technique. Focus on maintaining proper form and gradually increasing weights for progressive overload. Make sure to prioritize recovery and nutrition for the best results.
